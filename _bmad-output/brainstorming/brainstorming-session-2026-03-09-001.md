## Technique Execution Results

### SCAMPER Method

**Interactive Focus:** Systematically expanded the TaagBack concept through all seven SCAMPER lenses, with deepest exploration in Substitute, Combine, and Eliminate.

**Key Breakthroughs:**

**[Substitute #1]**: QR as Universal Experience Gateway
_Concept_: TaagBack isn't limited to scavenger hunts — QR codes become a flexible delivery mechanism for any sequential or location-based experience: restaurant menus, museum tours, event programs, product instructions, ad campaigns, show listings, and more.
_Novelty_: Most QR platforms are single-purpose. TaagBack treats the QR code as a programmable interaction point that creators can fill with any experience type.

**[Substitute #2]**: Intelligent QR Middleware Platform
_Concept_: TaagBack positions itself as the smart layer between the physical QR code and the digital experience — adding sequencing, analytics, dynamic content, interactivity, and creator tools on top of what would otherwise be a dumb link.
_Novelty_: Competitors offer one of these features. TaagBack bundles them all into a creator-driven experience platform.

**[Substitute #3]**: Context-Aware Dynamic QR
_Concept_: The same physical QR code delivers different content based on context — time of day, number of previous scans, user profile, weather, event state.
_Novelty_: Most QR solutions are static link redirects. Dynamic context-awareness turns a printed code into a living, responsive touchpoint.

**[Substitute #4]**: Auto-Generated & AI-Assembled Experiences **[FUTURE VISION]**
_Concept_: The platform could auto-generate experiences from templates based on business type, or let players create experiences for others by tagging locations. AI assembles flows so creators don't start from scratch.
_Novelty_: Removes the creator burden entirely — goes from "build your experience" to "we built it for you, just print the codes."

**[Combine #1]**: Gamification as Core Layer
_Concept_: Every QR experience type gets gamification baked in — not just scavenger hunts but any use case. Scan all menu items at a restaurant? Achievement. Hit every exhibit at a museum? Badge.
_Novelty_: Gamification isn't bolted on as a feature — it's the fundamental reason TaagBack codes are more engaging than a plain QR link.

**[Combine #2]**: QR Scan + Social Sharing
_Concept_: After scanning, players can share progress, achievements, or discoveries with friends. Turns solitary scanning into a social activity that markets the experience organically.
_Novelty_: The scan becomes inherently shareable, giving creators free viral distribution.

**[Combine #3]**: Serendipitous Discovery Model
_Concept_: QR codes live in the real world as ambient, discoverable touchpoints. Users encounter a code organically, scan it, get intrigued, and get pulled into the experience. The real world becomes the game board.
_Novelty_: Flips the typical app-first model. The QR code IS the user acquisition channel.

**[Combine #4]**: Geocaching Meets QR — Passive Discovery Network
_Concept_: Two distinct user archetypes — the Adventurer (geocaching types who actively seek out codes) and the Wanderer (casual stroller who spots a code and gets curious). Both are valid entry points.
_Novelty_: Unlike geocaching which requires intent and a dedicated app upfront, TaagBack codes are scannable by any phone camera through the TaagBack app. Zero friction discovery for Wanderers, with depth for Adventurers.

**[Combine #5]**: QR Code Overlay / Repurposing Layer — THE CORE PREMISE
_Concept_: TaagBack is a parallel layer on top of the world's existing QR code infrastructure. Any QR code — active, defunct, commercial, random — can be "claimed" and repurposed within TaagBack. The app becomes a lens that reveals a hidden world layered on top of everyday QR codes. Creators don't need to print new codes — they build experiences using codes that already exist in the physical world.
_Novelty_: No one is doing this. Every QR platform assumes you're generating new codes. TaagBack treats the billions of existing QR codes as a pre-built game board.

**[Combine #6]**: Taag Collection System
_Concept_: Every QR code scanned through TaagBack becomes a "Taag" in your collection. Even outside of organized hunts, users can collect Taags — building a personal map of every code they've encountered.
_Novelty_: Turns the mundane act of scanning any QR code into a collectible moment.

**[Combine #7]**: Native App as the Magic Lens
_Concept_: The TaagBack app is required to see the TaagBack layer. Scanning a QR code with your normal camera gives you whatever that code originally pointed to. Scanning it with TaagBack reveals the hidden game layer. Two realities for the same code.
_Novelty_: Creates an "in the know" dynamic — TaagBack users see a secret world that non-users walk right past.

**[Combine #8]**: Capture the Flag Mode
_Concept_: Teams compete to scan specific QR codes first, claiming them for their team. Physical QR codes become contested territory.
_Novelty_: Brings a competitive physical-world team game mechanic into the QR space.

**[Combine #9]**: First Claimer / Taag Pioneer System
_Concept_: The first person to scan a legacy QR code into TaagBack gets permanent credit — their name attached forever, plus the right to give it a special name. A digital plaque on a physical-world artifact.
_Novelty_: Creates a land-rush dynamic. Like planting a flag on undiscovered territory.

**[Combine #10]**: Taag Hunter Leaderboards
_Concept_: Leaderboards ranking users by most Taags claimed/added in a given time period — weekly, monthly, all-time. Creates friendly competition and recurring engagement.
_Novelty_: Combines geocaching discovery motivation with competitive leaderboard mechanics.

**[Combine #11]**: Named Taags — User-Generated Landmarks
_Concept_: When you claim a legacy QR code, you get to name it. These names become part of the TaagBack map and lore. A user-generated layer of landmarks on top of the real world.
_Novelty_: Creates emergent folklore and world-building. Players aren't just collecting — they're naming the world.

**[Combine #12]**: Taag Claim Maintenance — Monthly Re-Scan
_Concept_: To keep your name and custom name on a claimed Taag, you must physically re-scan it at least once a month. If you lapse, the Taag becomes unclaimed and someone else can claim it.
_Novelty_: Forces ongoing physical-world engagement, prevents squatting/hoarding, ensures claimed Taags are tied to active users. Creates natural turnover and recurring "land rush" moments.

**[Combine #13]**: Taag Identity vs Taag Function — Dual Layer
_Concept_: A Taag has two independent layers. The identity layer (who claimed it, its custom name, pioneer credit) is the collectible/discovery game. The function layer (what happens when you scan it in a hunt) is controlled by whoever built that experience. Losing a claim doesn't break hunts that use that Taag.
_Novelty_: Decouples collection mechanics from experience mechanics. Clean, resilient game design.

**[Modify #1]**: Scalable Hunt Geography
_Concept_: Hunts can range from hyper-local (a single building) to city-wide to multi-city epic adventures. The creator chooses the scale.
_Novelty_: Same platform, wildly different scales.

**[Modify #2]**: Micro-Interactions vs Rich Content
_Concept_: Taag interactions exist on a spectrum — from a 3-second animation to a full story chapter or puzzle. Creators mix and match density within a single experience. Pacing becomes a creative tool.
_Novelty_: Not every stop needs to be heavy. A hunt could have 20 quick-hit Taags with a few rich "boss" Taags mixed in.

**[Modify #3]**: Taag Profile Card — Auto-Generated Identity
_Concept_: Every claimed Taag gets a system-generated profile card featuring: custom name, claimer's username, location context, and something unique generated by AI (fun fact, poetic description, historical tidbit).
_Novelty_: Gen AI turns every mundane QR code location into something with personality and story.

**[Modify #4]**: Taag as Gateway Funnel
_Concept_: Every standalone Taag scan is a soft onboarding touchpoint. New user? "Start collecting?" Existing collector? "Added to your collection!" Then a gentle prompt to explore nearby hunts. Every scan is an entry point deeper into the ecosystem.
_Novelty_: No dead-end scans. Every interaction either starts a journey or deepens one.

**[Modify #5]**: Feature Catalog Discovery via Taags
_Concept_: Standalone Taag scans surface different game modes organically. The platform's feature catalog is discovered through play, not through a settings menu.
_Novelty_: Features are revealed contextually at the moment they're most relevant. Low floor revealed naturally.

**[Put to Other Uses #1]**: Educational Campus Hunts
_Concept_: Teachers build curriculum-tied scavenger hunts using QR codes already on school property — library books, lab equipment, bulletin boards.
_Novelty_: Zero setup cost for schools — the codes are already there.

**[Put to Other Uses #2]**: Self-Guided Property Tours
_Concept_: Real estate agents link existing property listing QR codes into a self-guided tour experience.
_Novelty_: Repurposes existing real estate signage QR codes.

**[Put to Other Uses #3]**: Conference & Event Gamification
_Concept_: Event organizers turn the vendor floor into a game. Every booth already has a QR code — TaagBack layers a collection challenge on top.
_Novelty_: Solves the age-old conference problem of unvisited booths.

**[Put to Other Uses #4]**: City Tourism & Downtown Exploration
_Concept_: Tourism boards build "Explore Downtown" experiences using existing merchant QR codes.
_Novelty_: Cities don't install anything. The infrastructure is already there.

**[Put to Other Uses #5]**: Corporate Team Building
_Concept_: Companies use TaagBack for team building events — competitive hunts around an office park, city block, or conference venue.
_Novelty_: No expensive scavenger hunt vendor needed.

**[Eliminate #1]**: The Three Pillars — MVP Core Definition
_Concept_: TaagBack's MVP rests on three pillars:
1. **Hunt Builder** — Add existing real-world QR codes into a sequence with clues. The core scavenger hunt.
2. **Taag Leaderboard & Databank** — Competitive sourcing, claiming, scanning. Builds TaagBack's most valuable asset — a crowdsourced QR code database.
3. **Retention Mechanic** — Monthly re-scan to maintain claims. Keeps people coming back.
_Novelty_: Three pillars, each feeding the others in a tight loop.

**[Eliminate #2]**: The Flywheel These Three Create
_Concept_: Pillar 2 builds the database. Pillar 1 gives it purpose. Pillar 3 keeps people feeding both. The crowdsourced Taag databank is the hidden strategic asset.
_Novelty_: Users think they're playing a game — they're actually building a global QR code registry.

**[Eliminate #3]**: MVP Retention — Re-Scan to Maintain
_Concept_: The re-scan maintenance model is the MVP retention mechanic. Simple, elegant, forces real-world engagement.
_Novelty_: The simplest possible retention loop that creates urgency and drives recurring physical-world activity.

**[Eliminate #4]**: Future — Challenge Gyms at Designated Locations **[FUTURE VISION]**
_Concept_: Special permanent QR codes designated by the system as Challenge Locations with competitive gym-style play. Unlike regular Taags which use passive re-scan maintenance, these are active PvP territory.
_Novelty_: Two-tier Taag system — peaceful claiming for regular Taags, competitive play for designated locations.

**[Reverse #1]**: Passive Hunt Creation — Player Becomes Creator
_Concept_: As players scan Taags organically, TaagBack detects patterns and prompts: "You scanned 5 Taags along Main Street — want to turn your route into a hunt?" The player just adds clues and publishes.
_Novelty_: Demolishes the creator/player divide. Creation happens as a byproduct of play. Ultimate low floor into creation.

**[Reverse #2]**: Scan History as Content
_Concept_: Your scan history itself becomes shareable content — a trail of everywhere you've been. Other users can "follow in your footsteps" by retracing your scan path.
_Novelty_: Every player is passively generating content for the platform just by using it.

### Role Playing

**Interactive Focus:** Embodied four key personas — the 14-year-old first user, the hunt creator, and TaagBack as a business. Deep UX journey mapping through the first-user experience.

**Key Breakthroughs:**

**[Role Play - First User #1]**: The First Scan Delight Moment
_Concept_: First-ever Taag scan triggers celebratory animation — confetti, fanfare. "You're the FIRST person to bring this Taag into TaagBack!" Immediate dopamine hit tied to pioneer status.
_Novelty_: The celebration is specifically because you're the PIONEER, not just a generic welcome.

**[Role Play - First User #2]**: AI-Assisted Naming with Location Context
_Concept_: GPS + Gen AI generates contextual name suggestions based on nearby businesses and landmarks. Fun, contextual, immediate — but the user can always go custom.
_Novelty_: The AI uses real-world location context to create names that feel connected to the place.

**[Role Play - First User #3]**: Content Moderation on Custom Names
_Concept_: Custom name input runs through a content filter. Crude names get rejected with a playful nudge — "Nice try! How about something your grandma would approve of?"
_Novelty_: Essential for younger users. Playful tone, not scolding.

**[Role Play - First User #4]**: Neighborhood Identity in Naming
_Concept_: Users naturally apply local knowledge — neighborhood nicknames, street lore, inside jokes. Over time TaagBack's Taag names become a folk map of how locals actually think about their neighborhoods.
_Novelty_: Cultural artifact disguised as a game feature.

**[Role Play - First User #5]**: Retention Hook Delivered In-Context
_Concept_: The monthly re-scan requirement is introduced at peak emotional investment — right after you named YOUR Taag. It doesn't feel like a chore, it feels like protecting your territory.
_Novelty_: Retention mechanic introduced at maximum emotional investment.

**[Role Play - First User #6]**: Already Claimed — The "Darn" Moment
_Concept_: Scanning an already-claimed Taag triggers a playful "darn!" animation. Shows the current claimer's name. Still adds the Taag to your collection. The loss is softened by a win.
_Novelty_: Emotional design — the "darn" is theatrical, not punishing. You still GET something.

**[Role Play - First User #7]**: Watchlist / Favorites — Contested Taag Alerts
_Concept_: Add any claimed Taag to a favorites/watchlist. Get notified when it becomes uncontested (claimer didn't re-scan). First to scan after expiry gets to reclaim and rename.
_Novelty_: Turns a dead-end moment into an ongoing engagement hook. Creates a "sniper" dynamic.

**[Role Play - First User #8]**: Taag Credit History — Three-Tier Attribution
_Concept_: Every Taag displays three distinct roles:
1. **Original Discoverer** — permanent, never changes
2. **Current Controller** — maintains through monthly re-scans
3. **Custom Name** — set by current controller, changes with control changes
_Novelty_: Original Discoverer credit is PERMANENT. Early adopters are rewarded forever.

**[Role Play - First User #9]**: Hunt Discovery Through Taag Scan
_Concept_: When a scanned Taag is part of an active hunt, the Taag card shows everything it normally would PLUS a hunt invitation. Hunts find players, not the other way around.
_Novelty_: Low floor discovery — you don't browse a catalog, you stumble into hunts in the real world.

**[Role Play - First User #10]**: Safety First — Contextual TOU Gate
_Concept_: Before any activity involving traveling to physical locations, a safety reminder appears with TOU link. First-time players MUST accept. Subsequent times show reminder only.
_Novelty_: Safety gate appears at the moment it's relevant, not buried during account creation.

**[Role Play - First User #11]**: Clue Presentation and Progression
_Concept_: After accepting a hunt, the player receives clues. Solve it to find the next QR code. Simple, tactile loop: clue → think → walk → scan → reward → next clue.
_Novelty_: Ancient game loop (scavenger hunts) with modern medium. The phone is the game master, the real world is the board.

**[Role Play - First User #12]**: No Progress Counter — Preserve the Mystery
_Concept_: Deliberately omit a "3 of 5" progress indicator during active hunts. The uncertainty IS the excitement. You're on an adventure, not completing a task bar.
_Novelty_: Counter-intuitive UX that prioritizes emotional experience over information.

**[Role Play - First User #13]**: Hunt Map — Post-Scan Breadcrumbs **[FUTURE]**
_Concept_: A map showing where you've already found clues in the current hunt. Retrospective, not predictive. Shows where you've been, not where to go.
_Novelty_: Preserves discovery while giving a sense of journey.

**[Role Play - First User #14]**: Subtle Social Presence — "Others Are Here"
_Concept_: A vague, ambient indicator that other players are also on this hunt. No count, no names, no details. Just a whisper that you're not alone.
_Novelty_: Threading the needle between social proof and bubble-popping.

**[Role Play - First User #15]**: Design Principle — "Protect the Magic"
_Concept_: TaagBack's UX decisions consistently prioritize wonder, discovery, and personal ownership over data, metrics, and social transparency. When in doubt, show less, not more.
_Novelty_: Product-wide design filter alongside "low floor, high ceiling." Every feature gets asked: "Does this protect the magic or break the spell?"

**[Role Play - First User #16]**: Hunt Completion — The Blackout Crescendo
_Concept_: Upon scanning the final Taag, the screen goes dramatically black. A pause. Then a celebratory fanfare fades in and CRESCENDOS with confetti, music, and fireworks. TaagBack's signature moment.
_Novelty_: Most apps give a checkmark. TaagBack gives a MOVIE MOMENT. The dramatic pause is emotional design at its best.

**[Role Play - First User #17]**: Creator's Completion Message
_Concept_: After the fanfare, the hunt creator's custom completion message is displayed. A personal voice from the person who built this experience.
_Novelty_: Direct emotional connection between creator and player.

**[Role Play - First User #18]**: Hunt Completion Stats & Leaderboard Introduction
_Concept_: After celebration, reveals hunt completion stats and introduces the hunt completion leaderboard — a second competitive dimension alongside Taag sourcing.
_Novelty_: Two leaderboard axes — famous for FINDING Taags or for COMPLETING hunts. Different play styles, different paths to status.

**[Role Play - Creator #1]**: Draft-as-You-Go Hunt Creation
_Concept_: Creator taps "Create Hunt," fills in name/metadata, hunt enters draft state. As they explore the world, they scan Taags and add them to the draft, placing them in sequence and writing clues on-site.
_Novelty_: Creation happens IN the field. The creator experiences the player's future journey as they build it.

**[Role Play - Creator #2]**: Map-Based Hunt Builder
_Concept_: Creator opens a map view showing all sourced Taags in an area. Through a clean interface, they tap Taags to add to a sequence, arrange order, and write clues — all from the couch.
_Novelty_: Leverages the crowdsourced Taag databank as a creation tool. The more Taags sourced, the richer the creator's palette.

**[Role Play - Creator #3]**: Gen AI Clue Assistant
_Concept_: Gen AI suggests clues based on Taag location data, name, nearby landmarks, and enrichment data. Lowers the creative barrier for hunt creators.
_Novelty_: AI assistance means anyone can create engaging hunts, not just wordsmiths. Low floor for creation.

**[Role Play - Creator #4]**: Taag Enrichment at Sourcing — Location Context Capture **[FUTURE]**
_Concept_: When sourcing a new Taag, users optionally add enrichment — text descriptions, photos of surroundings, short video clips. This data feeds Taag profile cards AND powers AI clue generation.
_Novelty_: Every sourced Taag becomes richer over time. Enrichment data makes the entire platform smarter.

**[Role Play - Creator #5]**: Enrichment Feeds the Flywheel
_Concept_: Sourcers add context → AI generates better clues → Creators build better hunts → Players have better experiences → More players become sourcers → More enrichment. Each data layer makes every other feature more powerful.
_Novelty_: The platform gets smarter with every interaction through community enrichment.

**[Role Play - Creator #6]**: Hybrid Creation — Start on Map, Finish in Field
_Concept_: Creator starts in map view, picks promising Taags, drops them into a rough sequence. Then walks the route in person — confirming codes exist, reordering for flow, adding new discoveries, writing clues on-site.
_Novelty_: Planning efficiency of map-based creation with authenticity of in-field building.

### What If Scenarios

**Interactive Focus:** Explored platform architecture, data intelligence, growth mechanics, social features, trust/safety, and scale challenges.

**Key Breakthroughs:**

**[What If #1]**: PWA / Web App as Alternative Entry Point
_Concept_: A Progressive Web App version for lower-friction first-time entry. Not a replacement for native, but a funnel top.
_Novelty_: Reduces the biggest friction point — app download — for Wanderer types.

**[What If #2]**: The Interception Problem — Why TaagBack Must Be the Scanner
_Concept_: TaagBack's core value requires intercepting QR codes before they resolve to their original destination. The app reads raw encoded data and uses it as a unique identifier. This technical constraint IS the competitive moat.
_Novelty_: The native app requirement is a feature, not a limitation. TaagBack owns the scanning experience.

**[What If #3]**: TaagBack-Created QR Codes — The Bridge Exception
_Concept_: QR codes generated within TaagBack encode a TaagBack URL directly — scannable by any camera. These are the gateway that gets people to install the app. Deep-links into app or app store.
_Novelty_: Solves the cold-start discovery problem. TaagBack-created codes are the gateway drug to the hidden layer.

**[What If #4]**: TaagBack-Created Codes — Platform Marketing Tool, Not User Requirement
_Concept_: TaagBack-generated codes are for the COMPANY's marketing use. Users CAN create them but the platform never requires it. Real growth engine is organic.
_Novelty_: Avoids building features around user behaviors that won't happen (printing).

**[What If #5]**: Shareable Moments as Viral Engine
_Concept_: The completion fanfare, first Taag discovery, rare finds — all inherently shareable moments. Share functionality at every peak moment. The content is the marketing.
_Novelty_: TaagBack doesn't need a referral program. It needs moments so delightful users WANT to share them.

**[What If #6]**: Deep Link Sharing — No Print Required
_Concept_: Creators share hunts via regular links — text, social media, group chat. The link deep-links into the app. The first clue tells you where to go find the first real-world QR code.
_Novelty_: Digital sharing replaces physical printing. The hunt starts in your messages and pulls you into the real world.

**[What If #7]**: Taag Expiration — Use It or Lose It
_Concept_: If a Taag goes unscanned for a defined period (e.g., 6 months), it expires. Natural decay keeps the databank healthy and accurate.
_Novelty_: Self-healing database. Community naturally prunes dead Taags through inactivity.

**[What If #8]**: Mobile/Transient Code Detection — Location Drift Flagging
_Concept_: When a Taag is sourced, GPS is recorded. If the same code is later scanned at a significantly different location, the system flags it. Codes on cars, trucks, or moving objects get invalidated.
_Novelty_: Proactive data integrity. TaagBack's location-anchored model REQUIRES stationary codes.

**[What If #9]**: Duplicate Code Detection — The Snack Wrapper Problem
_Concept_: Mass-produced QR codes (product packaging, chain restaurants) all encode the same URL. Same encoded data + multiple distinct GPS locations = mass-produced code = invalid.
_Novelty_: Critical data quality filter. Algorithmic and automatable.

**[What If #10]**: QR Encoded Data as Intelligence Source
_Concept_: TaagBack's backend crawls the encoded URL to learn: what business, what industry, active or defunct? Metadata enriches Taag profiles automatically AND builds a massive structured dataset.
_Novelty_: The QR code tells you about itself if you bother to look. Automatic context enrichment at zero user cost.

**[What If #11]**: The Data Goldmine — Aggregated QR Intelligence **[FUTURE]**
_Concept_: At scale, TaagBack builds the world's most comprehensive database of physical QR code deployments. Valuable to retail analytics, city planners, marketing agencies, commercial real estate.
_Novelty_: Users unknowingly build a real-world commercial intelligence dataset by playing. Even if not monetized at MVP, capture this data from day one.

**[What If #12]**: Auto-Categorized Taag Types
_Concept_: Backend analyzes encoded URLs to auto-categorize each Taag — Restaurant, Retail, Ghost (defunct), Municipal, Transit, Art/Culture, etc. Each category has distinctive visual style on the Taag card.
_Novelty_: Taags aren't generic pins — they have species. The world feels richer without user effort.

**[What If #13]**: Category-Based Collection Badges & Titles
_Concept_: Collecting Taags of specific types unlocks themed badges: 15 Restaurants → "Foodie Explorer," 3 Ghosts → "Digital Archaeologist," 20 in one neighborhood → "Local Legend."
_Novelty_: Collection becomes multi-dimensional. Players develop emergent play styles and identities.

**[What If #14]**: Ghost Taags — A Special Class
_Concept_: Defunct business QR codes become "Ghost Taags" with unique spooky visual treatment. The older and deader the business, the rarer the ghost. Collector's items.
_Novelty_: Turns urban decay into treasure. Kids will SEEK OUT abandoned businesses.

**[What If #15]**: Category Rarity Based on Local Density
_Concept_: Rarity is local, not global. A restaurant Taag downtown is common. A restaurant Taag in a rural area is rare. System calculates rarity by category density in the geographic area.
_Novelty_: Encourages exploration beyond obvious hotspots. Suburbs and rural areas have the rare finds.

**[What If #16]**: Player Profiles & Follow System
_Concept_: Every user has a profile with stats. Follow other players and see their activity. The kid who sources 500 Taags becomes locally famous.
_Novelty_: Turns anonymous scanners into community members with reputations.

**[What If #17]**: Local Taag Activity Feed
_Concept_: A feed showing recent Taag activity in your area — newly sourced, recently unclaimed, new hunts published. Like a neighborhood bulletin board for the TaagBack world.
_Novelty_: Ambient awareness that pulls you back outside even when not actively hunting.

**[What If #18]**: Hunt Reviews & Ratings
_Concept_: After completing a hunt, players rate (1-5 stars) and review. Quality signal surfaces the best hunts. Creators get feedback.
_Novelty_: Creates a meritocracy — great creators rise to the top.

**[What If #19]**: Creator Profiles & Hunt Portfolios
_Concept_: Creators get profiles showing published hunts, ratings, plays, completion rates. Top creators earn titles like "Master Architect."
_Novelty_: Creator economy built on status and recognition.

**[What If #20]**: Social Sharing Integration
_Concept_: At every peak moment, a share button for TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat, group chat. Content is visually rich — designed to look amazing in a social feed.
_Novelty_: Every share is organic marketing. Curiosity drives installs.

**[What If #21]**: Trust & Safety — Lean Legal Framework
_Concept_: Follow Niantic precedent — robust TOS/TOU, in-app safety reminders, user reporting, standard content moderation. Invest in legal protection, don't over-engineer safety features.
_Novelty_: The legal groundwork IS the safety feature.

**[What If #22]**: Community Reporting System

## Design Principles Established

1. **Low Floor, High Ceiling** — Instant magic for new users, bottomless depth for power users. Complexity revealed, never imposed.

3. **The Three MVP Pillars** — Hunt Builder, Taag Leaderboard/Databank, Re-scan Retention. Everything else is future.
4. **The Flywheel** — Discovery Loop → Creation Loop → Play Loop → feeds back to Discovery.
