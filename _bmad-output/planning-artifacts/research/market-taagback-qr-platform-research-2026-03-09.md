### Competitive Threats — Deep Assessment

**Threat Level: HIGH — Big Tech Entry**
Apple's native camera QR scanning, Google Lens, and Snap's AR capabilities could theoretically build a QR overlay. However:
- Apple/Google are unlikely to build a gamified niche product (not their business model)
- Snap's AR efforts have been in visual AR, not QR code interaction
- Building the community/flywheel from scratch is hard even with distribution
- _Mitigation_: Move fast, build the Taag databank as a defensible data moat

**Threat Level: MEDIUM-HIGH — Niantic/Scopely Pivot**
Scopely (now owning Pokémon GO) could add QR scanning to their existing games.
- The $3.5B acquisition is focused on monetization optimization, not new mechanics
- Pokémon GO's brand is tied to creature-catching; a pivot would confuse the core audience
- Adding QR overlay to an existing game is architecturally complex
- _Mitigation_: TaagBack's QR overlay is the CORE product, not a bolt-on feature. Depth beats breadth.

**Threat Level: MEDIUM — QR Platform Companies Adding Gamification**
QR Tiger (850K brands) could add gamification.
- QR platforms are B2B tools with no consumer audience or app distribution
- Building gamification UX is a fundamentally different competency than code generation
- Their business model (selling codes to businesses) conflicts with repurposing existing codes
- _Mitigation_: TaagBack's consumer-first, gamification-first DNA is difficult to replicate as an afterthought

**Threat Level: LOW-MEDIUM — Scavenger Hunt Platforms Evolving**
GooseChase or Scavify could add QR overlay mechanics.
- Their architectures are built around organizer-created task lists, not open-world discovery
- They lack consumer audiences (B2B event tools)
- Adding open-world collection mechanics would require a complete platform rebuild
- _Mitigation_: TaagBack's three-pillar architecture is designed from the ground up for this model
